Define your severity, P1, and P2 scales and risk acceptability thresholds below. Per ISO 14971 §4, these criteria must be established — and this plan approved — before hazard identification begins. Thresholds must be grounded in state-of-the-art guidance for your device type, not adjusted to make risks look acceptable after the fact.
Risk Profile Number (RPN) = Severity × P1 × P2, where P1 is the probability of the hazardous situation occurring and P2 is the conditional probability of harm given the hazardous situation (ISO/TR 24971 §B.3). Maximum RPN = 5 × 5 × 5 = 125.

GUIDANCE Risk Management Plan
ISO 14971:2019 §4 requires that a risk management plan be prepared and maintained for each medical device. It must be approved before hazard identification begins — you cannot define acceptability criteria after you've seen the risks.
Required plan elements (§4.2)
a) Scope of the risk management activities
b) Assignment of responsibilities and authorities
c) Requirements for review of risk management activities
d) Criteria for risk acceptability — including overall residual risk
e) Method for evaluating overall residual risk acceptability
P1 × P2 framework (ISO/TR 24971 §B.3)
P1 = probability that a sequence of events leads to a hazardous situation
P2 = conditional probability that harm results once the hazardous situation has occurred
POH = P1 × P2 (probability of harm)
RPN = Severity × P1 × P2 (max 125)
Note: ISO/TR 24971 clarifies that ALARP is a process, not a region. The middle zone is properly characterized as "Tolerable — Investigate Further."
